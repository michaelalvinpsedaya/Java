## Object-oriented Programming

Mapping real-world objects and relationships into programs to solve practical problems is the way of thinking in programming. Viewing entities in the objective world as living "**objects**" with **attributes, behaviors, and relationships**... this is the mindset of object-oriented thinking.

<br>

## object-oriented vs procedural

- If you have learned the C language and developed the programming mindset of **step-by-step**, this **structured** programming thinking is procedural
- Object-oriented or procedural is not about having clear boundaries, but about different focuses. Object-oriented also includes procedural, such as `if else`, `for`, and other control statements
- Object-oriented seems "difficult," but it actually maps the real world into programming. Remember **don't understand the world through programs; understand programs through the real world**. Trust that the more you learn, the easier it becomes!
